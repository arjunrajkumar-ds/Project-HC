How It Works

A simple, repeatable system for getting stronger over time — built around three exercise tiers and a progression model that advances automatically as you improve.

{# ── Tier system ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

The Tier System

T1 Foundation Lifts

Heavy compound movements — squat, deadlift, bench press, overhead press, weighted pull-up. These are the engine of the programme. Each session opens with one or two T1 lifts performed at near-maximum intensity. Strength built here carries everywhere else.

T1 exercises rotate so each one gets hit regularly. The app tracks when you last did each lift and surfaces the one due next.

T2 Supporting Work

Assistance exercises that reinforce the T1 movements — dumbbell variations, unilateral work, barbell accessories. Two to three T2 exercises per session, selected to match the day type (Push, Pull, Legs, Core) so they complement what you just trained.

When logging, the app shows recommended T2 choices for your day type up top, with the full library below if you want something else.

T3 Detail Work

Isolation and aesthetic work — curls, lateral raises, cable movements, calf raises. Entirely optional, added at the end when you have energy left. No progression tracking; just pick weights that feel right and get a pump.

{# ── Linear progression ──────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Linear Progression

Every T1 exercise follows a progression ladder. The goal is simple: do the prescribed sets and reps, then advance. When you've worked through all the stages, add weight and start again.

Stage 1
3 × 3
Entry point — manageable volume at the new weight
Stage 2
3 × 4
Stage 3
3 × 5
Stage 4
4 × 3
Sets increase — same reps, more total volume
Stage 5
4 × 4
Stage 6
4 × 5
Stage 7
5 × 3
Stage 8
5 × 4
Stage 9
5 × 5
Final stage — add weight, reset to Stage 1
Adding weight: completing 5×5 means you've earned the next load. Increase the weight — typically 2.5 kg for upper body, 5 kg for squat and deadlift — and the cycle resets to 3×3. Each full cycle represents real, measurable progress.
{# ── Swimming ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Swimming

Swim sessions are logged separately and tracked by total distance. Log your sets and rep distance and the app calculates total metres swum. Swim volume is shown on the dashboard alongside your weekly lifting numbers, and each session appears in the activity heatmap.

{# ── Swim achievements ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Swim Challenges

Swim sessions can be attempted against a set of challenges. Each challenge has a fixed scheme — a number of sets at a given rep distance — and you log how you got on when you finish. Challenges unlock progressively: you have to complete the first before the next opens up.

Chain 1
10 × 50 m → 20 × 50 m → 30 × 50 m
1,500 m total at the top — complete each to unlock the next
Chain 2
10 × 100 m
1,000 m of longer reps
Pyramid
5 × 50 m → 5 × 100 m → 5 × 50 m
Mixed-distance pyramid, 1,000 m total
Challenges can be completed multiple times — the app tracks your total count. A session that doesn't quite hit the target is logged as a partial attempt rather than a failure, so you can see how close you got.
